Assignment 2: Interactive Visualization Exercise

Analysis:

On analysis, the Australian Open Match dataset has complete match details including players, winners and country. Comparing players of same country with their corresponding opponents can help us find a pattern.

Aspects Compared

The aspects that were compared for a match between 2 players - Aces, Average First Serve, Average Second Serve, Fast Serve, Double, Total, Error, Winner

  • Aces- Good serves missed by Opponent. It contributes more to the success of the match.
  • Fast Serve - A speed serve is harder for the opponent to return gives the player ace points.
  • Total points - On a particular game this gives us total of points won because of error by opponent and won by player. This value will be proportional to success/failure of the game
  • Error - Used to compare the mistakes. Will be useful to see where the player makes mistake and where they need improvements
Interactive Visualization:
The visualization provides a way to view the players country-wise and compare with his opponent
  • At top of the page, an world map representing the number of players country-wise is shown.
    • The strength of the color is directly proportional to the players count.
    • Horizontal legend depicts the player count and color strength
    • On mouse over - move over the map- Country name and count of players is shown in tooltip
  • On click, a division to compare every player with his corresponding players over the time is shown.
    • Left side of the division shows the list of the players for the selected country.
    • On click of any player, his corresponding opponents for every year is listed.
  • On selection of any opponent, a smoothed radar chart comparing the stats of both the player is shown.
  • On selection of every player it asks to select opponent and also on varying opponents, the radar chart updates dynamically.

Colors and plots
  • Cartogram showing number of players uses same color variation to show the strength. Range of blue color is chosen to make it look appealing.
  • Radarchart plot were smoothed to give aesthetic feel
  • Bright colors were used to show the comparison and legends were added to relate color of curve with the player being compared.
